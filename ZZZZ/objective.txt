Dev 1 Brief — Backend Infrastructure & Ingestion
Who you are and what you own
You are Dev 1 on a 3-person team building a CV reformatting SaaS. Your job is backend infrastructure — the foundation everything else runs on. You own the API layer, authentication, file storage, database, and the file extraction module that reads raw CV files.
You do not touch AI agents (Dev 2) or Word template rendering (Dev 3). But everything they build plugs into what you create.

What the product does
Companies in the international development and renewable energy sector submit expert CVs to donors like GIZ and World Bank for project bids. These CVs must follow strict donor-mandated formats. The product automates this — a user uploads a raw CV (PDF or DOCX), selects a target format (GIZ or World Bank), optionally provides a Terms of Reference document, and gets back a properly formatted Word document.
The full pipeline has 5 AI agents followed by a Word renderer. Your job is everything before and around that pipeline — receiving inputs, storing files, tracking job status, serving outputs.

Tech stack decisions already made
These are locked. Do not deviate:

Framework: FastAPI (Python)
Auth: Supabase Auth
Database: Supabase Postgres
File storage: Supabase Storage
Async: FastAPI BackgroundTasks (not Celery — that comes later at scale)
AI: OpenAI GPT-4o (used by Dev 2, not you)
Python: 3.10+


The one file you already have
models.py is complete and locked. It lives at the root of the backend directory. It contains the CVData Pydantic schema — the central data contract the whole system uses. You import from it but never modify it without agreement from all three developers.
